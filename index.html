Pre & Post Workout Nutrition
Precise nutrient timing for maximum performance and recovery.
⚡ Pre-Workout (6:30 AM — 30 min before gym)

What: Simple + complex carbs + moderate protein + low fat
Why: Carbs = fuel for intensity. Protein prevents catabolism.

Eat: 2 bananas + 3 eggs + 200ml milk
Avoid: High fat foods (slow digestion), raw vegetables, high fiber

Hydration: 500ml water on wake-up, 300ml before leaving for gym
Key: Eat exactly 30–45 min before training, not earlier

🔄 Post-Workout (9:00 AM — within 30 min)

What: High protein + high carbs + low fat
Why: Anabolic window — muscle glycogen replenishment + protein synthesis peak

Veg: Soya chunks 75g + Oats 80g + Curd 150g
Non-Veg: Chicken 150g + Oats 80g + Curd 150g

Hydration: 500–750ml water immediately post-workout + electrolytes (pinch of salt + lemon)
Key: Don't skip. This is the most important meal of your day.

Progressive Overload Plan
The #1 driver of muscle growth. Follow this system exactly.
📐 The Double Progression Method

Work within a rep range (e.g., 8–12). When you can do all sets at the TOP of the range with good form, increase weight by the smallest increment available (2.5–5 kg). Drop back to the bottom of the range. Progress again. This is slow, sustainable, proven muscle building.

📔 What To Track

Log every workout in a notebook or app: Date · Exercise · Weight · Sets × Reps · RPE. Review weekly. If you're stalling on a lift for 2+ weeks, check: sleep, protein intake, technique, recovery. Don't add weight until you earn it.

Recovery Plan
Recovery is where muscle is actually built. Training breaks it down. Sleep builds it back.
😴 Sleep Protocol

Target: 7.5–8 hours (not 6 hours from midnight).
Optimal window: 10:30 PM – 6:00 AM
Why it matters: 90% of GH (Growth Hormone) is released during deep sleep. Poor sleep = poor recovery, higher cortisol, and more fat gain.

Sleep hygiene: No screens 30 min before bed · Keep room dark and cool · 250ml milk before bed (casein protein = overnight muscle repair) · Same sleep/wake time daily including weekends

🔁 Recovery Strategies

Muscle soreness (DOMS): Normal in weeks 1–3. Light movement speeds recovery.
Active recovery (Sunday): 20-min walk + full stretching routine
Contrast therapy: Hot shower → Cold rinse for 30 sec (free, effective)
Deload week (Week 11): Same movements, 30% less weight, 2–3 sets. Mandatory. Do not skip.

Stress: High cortisol destroys gains. 5-min box breathing daily is non-negotiable.

Beer Guide: Fitting It In Without Ruining Progress
Honest science-based breakdown of how alcohol affects your goals.
🍺 How Beer Affects Muscle Gain

Testosterone suppression: Alcohol reduces testosterone production for 12–24 hours post-consumption — the anabolic hormone you need most. Even moderate amounts suppress protein synthesis by ~15–20% for up to 8 hours. One beer session per week has minimal long-term impact if you plan it correctly.

🍺 How Beer Affects Fat Loss

Alcohol = empty calories: 1 bottle (650ml) beer ≈ 220–280 kcal. Alcohol is prioritized as fuel by your liver, which pauses fat oxidation for 1–3 hours. It also increases appetite and reduces inhibitions around food choices. On a 1,920 kcal target, 2 beers = ~500 kcal — 26% of your entire daily allowance.

✅ How to Fit Beer Without Ruining Progress

Best day: Saturday or Sunday (furthest from most important workouts)
Before drinking: Eat a high-protein meal first (chicken + roti). Slows absorption.
During: Limit to 1–2 standard drinks (330ml bottles). Drink 1 glass water per beer.
After: Eat 2 boiled eggs + roti before sleeping (protein before bed counters anti-anabolic effect)

Cardiovascular Health Assessment
Why you get breathless and how to fix it safely.
⚠️ Possible Causes of Breathlessness

1. Deconditioned cardiovascular system — Most likely cause. Low stroke volume, poor capillary density, reduced oxygen utilization.
2. Excess body fat (~22–26%) — More mass = more oxygen demand for same movement.
3. Poor breathing mechanics — Shallow chest breathing (not diaphragmatic).
4. Low activity levels (sedentary) — Body has adapted to minimal exertion.
5. Possible mild iron deficiency — Common in vegetarians. Iron carries oxygen in blood.

🚨 See a Doctor If...

You experience breathlessness at complete rest · Chest pain or tightness during or after exercise · Heart palpitations or irregular heartbeat · Dizziness or lightheadedness during mild activity · Breathlessness that gets worse over 4 weeks despite training · Any time you're concerned — better safe than sorry. Get a basic blood panel (CBC, iron, thyroid) if breathlessness persists beyond 8 weeks of training.
